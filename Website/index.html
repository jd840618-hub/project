Real World Uses

Jewelry & Automotive: Platinum is used extensively for jewellery. Its main use, however, is in catalytic converters for cars, trucks and buses. This accounts for about 50% of demand each year. Platinum is very effective at converting emissions from the vehicle's engine into less harmful waste products.
Industrial Catalyst: Platinum is used in the chemicals industry as a catalyst for the production of nitric acid, silicone and benzene. It is also used as a catalyst to improve the efficiency of fuel cells.

Interesting Facts

1. Platinum is a dense, malleable, and ductile metal with a silvery-white color.
2. Platinum is 30 times rarer than gold.
3. Platinum is 60 times rarer than silver.
4. The word "platinum" comes from the Spanish term "platina," which means "little silver."

Atomic Structure

Platinum atoms have 78 protons and 78 electrons. The electrons move around the nucleus in different energy levels called shells. Platinum has 6 electron shells with electrons arranged as: 2, 8, 18, 32, 17, 1. The nucleus contains 78 protons (positive) and 117 neutrons (neutral), while the 78 electrons (negative) orbit around it.
